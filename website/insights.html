Key Insights: How LLMs Perform on Deep Learning Homework

Overall Performance Patterns

📝

Mathematical Derivations

Most LLMs perform well on structured mathematical problems with clear steps. Models like Claude Opus 4.5, GPT 5.1, and DeepSeek often one-shot algebraic derivations correctly.

🤔

Conceptual Questions

LLMs tend to struggle more with intuition-based questions, often giving "first plausible" explanations that require human pushback to correct.

⚠

Confidence vs. Correctness

A recurring pattern is that models sound confident even when reasoning is incomplete or incorrect. They often hallucinate intermediate steps.

Common Strengths

  • Strong performance on linear algebra and matrix calculus
  • Good at following step-by-step derivation patterns
  • Useful for brainstorming proof structures and confirming intuition
  • Effective at explaining well-known results and concepts
  • Models with "thinking" modes show improved reasoning transparency

Common Weaknesses

  • Subtle Math Errors: Missing constants, incorrect simplifications, unjustified assumptions
  • PDF Parsing Issues: Some models struggle to correctly interpret mathematical notation from images
  • Hallucination: Models make up intermediate steps or explanations when unsure
  • Context Length: Longer problems cause format adherence decline and reasoning compression
  • Visual Analysis: Models tend to "guess" based on domain knowledge rather than analyzing actual data

LLM-Specific Observations

Claude (Sonnet/Opus 4.5)

Excellent at algebraic reasoning and optimization problems. Extended thinking mode produces rigorous, paper-like solutions. Strong on mechanical reasoning, weaker on conceptual intuition without prompting.

GPT 5.1 (Pro/Thinking/Extended)

Impressive one-shot capabilities on many problems. Useful as a "pocket-TA" but requires fundamental understanding to verify outputs. Extended thinking mode shows clearer reasoning.

DeepSeek v3.2

Strong grasp of linear algebra and calculus concepts. Good chain-of-thought reasoning. Tendency to simplify qualitative analysis using numerical examples rather than general derivations.

Gemini (Pro 3 / Flash)

Consistently includes intermediate steps that other LLMs skip. Good at geometric interpretations. "Thinking" mode delivers interpretations clearly on first try.

Mistral

High accuracy (99%) on some assignments with minimal errors. Zero arithmetic errors on well-structured problems. Initially acts as pedagogical tutor rather than solution key.

Kimi K2

Strong performance on mathematical derivations. Good at structured problems with clear steps. Sometimes needs explicit prompting for detailed explanations.

Recommendations for Using LLMs on Homework

1

Use LLMs for brainstorming and verifying intuition, not as a replacement for understanding

2

Always verify mathematical derivations - subtle errors are common

3

Use "thinking" or "extended thinking" modes when available for better reasoning

4

Provide explicit instructions for output format and level of detail

5

When models struggle, break problems into smaller parts

6

Human oversight remains essential - "meaningful human guidance is required throughout"
